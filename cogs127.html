COGS 127 // DATA-DRIVEN UX/PRODUCT DESIGN

Pikmin Bloom Accessibility Redesign

Role

UX/UI Designer & Researcher

Timeline

March 2026 - June 2026

Impact

Redesigning AR gaming for the 1.3 billion people living with mobility impairments.

Teammates

Livianne Wong, Valerie Djatikusuma, and Neela Kolte

01. Background

Inclusion is a $2 Trillion Opportunity

Most fitness-based AR (augmented reality) games are built on a single metric: footsteps. If you don't walk "normally", you don't progress. These games tend to exclude the global disability market, a demographic with about $2 trillion in annual disposable income.


The Problem

Pikmin Bloom’s game interface relies on pedometer data and high-frequency tapping. For users with mobility aids (wheelchairs, walkers, etc.) or limited fine motor skills, the game isn't designed to accommodate them.


The Solution

We decided to pivot the game’s philosophy from "Motivation to Walk" to "Motivation to Move." By integrating distance-based tracking and motor-inclusive toggles, we redesigned the experience to be universally rewarding, ensuring that no player is left behind.

02. User Research & Insights

We observed users working through various interactive flows and identified key cognitive friction points. By mapping out clear Information Architecture frameworks, we developed low-fidelity wireframes that prioritized core user tasks while reducing screen clutter.

03. The Solution & Takeaways

The finalized system implements high-contrast structural hierarchy and dynamic UI elements. By connecting systematic design layouts with intentional user interaction pathways, we created an interface that reduces cognitive loading and optimizes overall task speed.
