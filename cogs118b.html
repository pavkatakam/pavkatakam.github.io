Comparison of Left/Right Facing Cars with K-means clustering

Role

Data Analyst, Researcher, & Coder

Timeline

September 2025 - December 2025

Teammates

Christopher Arias and Zach Ishida

01. BACKGROUND

AI Algorithms

Within the world of image classification, there are numerous AI algorithms being used to differentiate objects in digital images and classify them. Tools like PCA, CLIP, and K-means are very popular, all being used in our project today: comparing left and right facing car images to see if different AI algorithms can successfully differentiate between them.

3 Comparisons

Overall, we are going to compare the results of K-means clustering when applied to different sets of features. We will use K-means directly on the CLIP features and K-means on the PCA features using the optimal k (10). We will also use K-means on the first 2 principal components to test how much information is lost by reduction.

02. METHODS

Replication

We plan to replicate the study on the optimal predictions in everyday cognition, by changing the study to relate more to current society. Out of the 9 topics tested, we decided to test our participants on total movie grosses and movie runtimes, topics we believed our participants would have some sort of prior knowledge on. In addition, we also only tested 3 values of anchors instead of 5 as our study was replicated on a smaller scale.


Participants

We were able to get a total of 31 responses from our friends and family. Our participants mostly consist of a younger crowd, 19-22 years old with some 30+ year old outliers. Every participant filled out a Google form, beginning with an initial unbiased question choosing a number 1-3, a number which determined which anchors they would be making their decisions on.

03. RESULTS

Data Distributions

Our dataset contains a collection of 1 million movies from the TMDB database up to 2024. Based on the given data, the two graphs show the empirical distribution of both movie runtimes and movie gross values. From the graphs, the movie gross distribution seems to be right skewed, with the majority of the data centered around 0 to 1 million dollars. On the other hand, the movie runtime distribution seems to be normally distributed but slightly right skewed. Based on the dataset and the graphs, the average runtime seems to be around 90 to 110 minutes.

K-means Clusters on 2D PCA Features

We noticed that the yellow and purple clusters represent the 2 different directions our dataset of car images were facing. Since the clusters are very separate, this shows that the left/right direction is very strong in the CLIP features. The accuracy of 68.94% is very high, but the scatter plot with 2 different purple clusters shows where the 31.06% of errors occurred.

04. CONCLUSION

Findings

Overall, we learned that CLIP embeddings and feature extraction are useful when looking at visual features like a car angles. We also learned that PCA is useful at looking at CLIP features and analyzing the principal components. In addition, PCA also taught us that the number of components used isn’t really important, since 2 components and 10 components gave the same results.


Expansion

In the future, we could have extended our project by using the CLIP embeddings for text. For this project, we only used the CLIP embeddings for images, but if we were able to use text, it could have improved the accuracy of our model.
